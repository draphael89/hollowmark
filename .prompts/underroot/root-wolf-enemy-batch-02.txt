Create one enemy sprite source for a first-person party dungeon crawler.

Subject: Rootbitten Wolf, a gaunt wolf-shaped dungeon beast grown through with
wet roots, exposed red-black bark sinew, and old bone ridges. It should feel
hungry, cursed, and grounded, not cute or heroic.

Pose: grounded combat idle, full body visible, three-quarter front view,
readable silhouette, feet/contact point visible, head and shoulders dominant.
Keep all limbs connected to the main body shape; avoid dangling twig clusters
that separate into tiny islands at sprite scale.

Lighting: warm torch from front-left, cold blue rim from upper-right, dark
contact shadow below.

Style: moonlit oxblood dungeon manuscript; painterly pixel-art source for a
16-bit-inspired first-person dungeon RPG; high-contrast silhouette; deep indigo
shadows; bone-colored highlights; muted oxblood accents; damp low-fantasy
horror; limited-palette feeling; no clean modern UI; no text.

Background: flat dark desaturated blue-gray matte, visibly different from the
wolf's black body shadows, easy to remove. Do not use pure black behind the
body. Do not blend the rear legs into the background.

Use: source image will be background-removed, outlined only if needed,
palette-quantized, and downsampled to 96x128. It must read against both a
checkerboard alpha review and the approved Underroot combat background.

Avoid: multiple creatures, cropped limbs, separated twig hairs, thin transparent
leg gaps, cute cartoon, anime style, bright saturated colors, photorealistic
fur, modern objects, readable letters, symbols, logos, UI, or text.
